Merging File Revisions

See Also

The Merge command is useful for incorporating changes in earlier revisions into your working copy. You can merge revisions on different branches of the repository with your local working copy of a file or directory. You can also merge the difference between two earlier revisions with your local working copy.

To merge an earlier revision with your working copy:

  1. In the Versioning window, expand the file's node to reveal the subnodes for each revision and branch.
  2. Right-click the revision you want to merge into your working copy and choose Merge With Revision.

    The IDE incorporates any differences found in the revision into your local copy of the file. If merge conflicts occur, the file's status is updated to Merge Conflict to indicate this.

To merge the difference between two revisions with your working copy:

  1. In the Versioning window, expand the file's node to reveal the subnodes for each revision and branch.
  2. Control-click the two revision nodes whose changes you want to merge into your working copy.
  3. Right-click either of the revisions and choose Merge With Rev. Differences.

    The IDE incorporates any differences in the revisions into your local copy of the file. If merge conflicts occur, the file's VCS status changes to Merge Conflict to indicate this.
