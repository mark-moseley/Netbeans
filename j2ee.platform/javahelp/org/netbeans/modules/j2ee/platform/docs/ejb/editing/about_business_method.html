About Business Methods

See Also

A business method is a method inside an enterprise bean that performs some function and is available for any clients of the bean. Business methods are typically written in the bean class and registered in the bean's remote interface.

An entity bean represents a persistent business object whose data is stored in a database. The bean adds behavior specific to that data. Therefore, an entity bean's business methods are typically accessor and mutator methods that enable you to read and update information in a database that represents a business concept.

A session bean's business methods typically define tasks that the bean performs for clients. They are not necessarily associated with data in a database.

Business Methods in the IDE

In the IDE, you can generate business methods by right-clicking the bean node in the Projects window and choosing Add  Add Business Method. When you create an enterprise bean in the IDE, the IDE creates a special BeanNameRemoteBusiness interface in which it registers business methods. The BeanNameRemote interface extends the BeanNameRemoteBusiness and the bean class implements both the BeanNameRemote interface and the BeanNameRemoteBusiness interface.

The advantage of this approach is that it lets you separate the business logic from implementation logic, and that it lets you check at compile-time that your bean implements the given interfaces. If you want to register business methods directly in your remote interface, delete the BeanNameRemoteBusiness.java, delete BeanNameRemoteBusiness from the list of interfaces BeanNameRemote extends and from the list of interfaces the bean class implements.
