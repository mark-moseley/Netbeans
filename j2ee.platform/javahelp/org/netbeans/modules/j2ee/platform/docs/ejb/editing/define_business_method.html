Defining a Business Method

See Also

An entity bean represents a persistent business object whose data is stored in a database. The bean adds behavior specific to that data. Therefore, an entity bean's business methods are typically accessor and mutator methods that enable you to read and update information in a database that represents a business concept.

A session bean's business methods typically define tasks that the bean performs for clients. They are not necessarily associated with data in a database.

To define a business method for an enterprise bean:

  1. In the Projects window, expand the Enterprise JavaBeans node and the node for the EJB.
  2. Right-click enterprise bean's node and choose Add Business Method.

    You can also open the bean class and right-click anywhere inside the body of the class and choose Enterprise JavaBeans (EJB)  Add Business Method. This command is only enabled when you click within the body of the class (after the class declaration).

  3. In the Add Business Method dialog box, define the method signature:
    1. Name the method.
    2. Specify the return type.
    3. Specify the method parameters in the Parameters tab.
    4. Specify the exceptions that the method throws in the Exceptions tab.
  4. Click OK to create the method.

    The IDE adds the method signature to the remote interface and the method body to the bean class.

  5. Edit your new business method.
    When the IDE adds a method to the bean class, it does not save the class. Only when you compile or save the class is the new method saved to disk.
